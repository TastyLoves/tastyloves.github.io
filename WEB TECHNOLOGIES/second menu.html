Strawberry Tiramisu



Strawberry Tiramisu is my favourite summer dessert! It's sweet, lovely, and SO DELICIOUS. Layers of vanilla-flavoured mascarpone, strawberry jam-infused ladyfingers, and heaps of fresh, luscious strawberries. When I bring this simple dessert to a gathering, everyone always enjoys it.

This summer version of the iconic Italian dessert is created without alcohol, raw eggs, or coffee, so it's suitable for everyone! Don't get me wrong, I like true Italian Tiramisu; it's what I usually order when we visit an Italian restaurant or travel to Italy, but if I bring a dessert to a family gathering, I want everyone to be able to enjoy it.

Strawberries! Fruit from the heart.

How to make

  1. Wash strawberries, remove stems, and cut in half lengthwise. In a saucepan, combine the strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat until the strawberries are soft. Allow to cool after removing from the heat.

  2. Combine the cream, sugar, sweetened condensed milk, and vanilla extract in a mixing bowl.

  3. After the ingredients have been combined, add the mascarpone cheese and thoroughly combine everything again.

  4. Mix in the strawberry pulp obtained in the first step.

  5. Place the cookies in the bottom of a container of your choice and drizzle with the strawberry syrup. Place the strawberries on the sides of the plate.

  6. Fill the container halfway with the mixture and flatten the surface.

  7. Sprinkle powdered sugar and strawberry powder over the top of the tiramisu. Garnish with strawberries and mint if desired.
